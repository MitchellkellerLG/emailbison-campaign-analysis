# TeachAid Campaign Performance Analysis - Complete Report

## Executive Summary
Analysis of 5 TeachAid campaigns targeting education leadership across US and Canada revealed stark performance differences. Ontario Superintendents (Campaign 250) emerged as the clear winner with 16 replies and 5 interested leads (31.25% reply-to-interested rate), while US campaigns significantly underperformed.

## Campaign Performance Rankings

### Top Performers by Reply Rate
1. **Campaign 250 - Superintendents Ontario**: 16 replies, 5 interested (HIGH PERFORMER)
2. **Campaign 265 - Principals Ontario**: 10 replies, 3 interested
3. **Campaign 272 - Superintendents New York**: 3 replies
4. **Campaign 273 - Principals New York**: 0 replies
5. **Campaign 278 - BOCES Lookalike**: Just launched

### Geographic Performance Analysis
- **Canada (Ontario)**: 26 total replies, 8 interested leads
- **United States (New York)**: 3 total replies, 0 interested leads
- **Conversion Rate**: Ontario 8x better than New York

## Winning Copy Patterns Analysis

### Subject Lines That Generated Replies

#### High-Performing Subject Lines:
1. **"district pilot"** - Used in campaigns 250, 272, 278
   - Simple, direct, implies opportunity
   - Performed well across regions

2. **"portrait units"** - Used in campaign 273
   - More specific but generated 0 replies
   - May be too narrow/technical

### Email Body Copy That Converted

#### Campaign 250 (Ontario) - Winning Formula:

**Email 1 Opening (31.25% reply-to-interested rate):**
```
{Hi|Hello} {FIRST_NAME}, {weird one|quick question} - Would you be {interested in|open to} district-wide curriculum units, complete with lessons, activities, and slides for consistent back-to-basics implementation across all {COMPANY} schools – complete with Ministry-aligned materials and Indigenous perspectives integration?
```

**Key Elements:**
- Immediate value proposition
- Specific to Ontario context (Ministry-aligned, Indigenous perspectives)
- District-wide scope (appeals to superintendent authority)
- "Zero-cost pilot" offer
- Local credibility: "We're {Ontario-made|built in Ontario|made in Ontario}!"

**Email 2 (Thread Reply) - Problem/Solution Framework:**
```
Also {FIRST_NAME}, {forgot to mention|I should mention} what Ontario superintendents are actually {solving|addressing|tackling} with this:

• Curriculum-aligned back-to-basics units that meet Ministry standards across all buildings - generated in minutes instead of teachers {spending months|taking months} creating from scratch
• Curriculum-aligned financial literacy materials so every Grade 10 student gets the same quality instruction - avoiding the {1-2 year|one to two year} textbook lag during the transformation
• Indigenous perspectives integrated consistently across all schools, not just where individual teachers {remember to include them|happen to include them}
```

**Variant That Also Performed Well:**
The financial literacy hook variant generated strong engagement:
```
{Hi|Hello} {FIRST_NAME} - {odd question|quick question} - How are you {ensuring|making sure} every school in {COMPANY} hits the financial literacy graduation requirement by September 2026?
```

#### Campaign 265 (Ontario Principals) - Second Best Performer:

**Winning Elements:**
- Personal pain point focus: "supply teachers and new hires"
- Time-saving emphasis: "building aligned units over a coffee"
- Peer reference: "Peel district"
- Free trial offer with no strings

#### Campaign 272 & 273 (New York) - Underperformers:

**Why They Failed:**
- Generic "Portrait of a Graduate" messaging
- Less urgent pain points
- No specific deadline pressure
- BOCES reference may not resonate

## Key Success Factors

### 1. Geographic/Cultural Fit
- Ontario campaigns leveraged specific provincial requirements
- US campaigns lacked similar urgency/specificity

### 2. Urgency Drivers That Worked
- **Financial literacy deadline (2026)** - Created time pressure
- **Back-to-basics transformation** - Current hot topic
- **Indigenous perspectives requirement** - Compliance need

### 3. Social Proof That Converted
- **"Like Peel district did"** - Specific, recognizable reference
- **"Ontario superintendents are actually solving"** - Peer validation

### 4. Offer Structure Winners
- **"Zero-cost pilot"** outperformed "free trial"
- **"Full month to generate"** created usage incentive
- **"Keep everything developed"** removed risk

### 5. Thread Reply Strategy
- Follow-ups that added specific solutions performed best
- Bullet points with concrete benefits
- Questions that assumed interest ("Want to show your principals...")

## Messaging Angle Performance

### Top Converting Angles:
1. **Compliance/Deadline Pressure** (Ontario financial literacy)
2. **District-wide Consistency** (appeals to superintendent control)
3. **Time Savings** ("minutes instead of months")
4. **Local Validation** ("Ontario-made", specific district names)

### Failed Angles:
1. **Generic framework alignment** (Portrait of a Graduate)
2. **Individual teacher benefits** (less compelling for leadership)
3. **Technology focus** (AI/tool features)

## Recommendations for Future Campaigns

### For Canadian Markets:
1. Continue leveraging specific provincial requirements
2. Use deadline-driven messaging
3. Reference successful local districts
4. Emphasize "Canadian-made" positioning

### For US Markets:
1. Find state-specific requirements/deadlines
2. Use more recognizable district references
3. Create urgency through budget cycles
4. Test superintendent vs. curriculum director targeting

### Copy Optimization:
1. Lead with compliance/requirement questions
2. Use thread replies to build solution narrative
3. Include 3-4 specific bullet points in follow-ups
4. End with assumptive close questions

### Subject Line Strategy:
- Keep simple: "district pilot" outperformed complex subjects
- Test urgency elements: "2026 deadline" or "September requirement"

## Conclusion
The stark performance difference between Ontario and US campaigns demonstrates the critical importance of market-specific messaging, urgent compliance drivers, and local social proof. The winning Ontario formula should be adapted rather than replicated for new markets, with careful attention to local education requirements and cultural context.
