## Understanding EmailBison Engagement Metrics

EmailBison does **not** have a separate "engaged" field. Instead, engagement is tracked through:

1. **Interested Leads** - Manually qualified leads marked as high-intent (conversion-ready)
2. **Unique Replies** - All leads who replied (broader engagement indicator)
3. **Total Engagement** - Combined unique replies metric showing all responsive leads

**Key Insight:** Only ~23% of repliers convert to "interested" status, meaning **interested leads are the highest quality subset** of all engaged respondents. The original report focused on "interested" counts, but below we also analyze campaigns by total reply volume to show the complete engagement picture.

- **Problem-solution bridge:** Acknowledge SEO dip ‚Üí Reddit opportunity
- **Competitor framing:** "your competitors are capturing this traffic"
- **AI/LLM angle:** "visitors from LLMs", "LLM citations"

### 4. Social Proof Patterns
- **Big brand names:** Blizzard, Afterpay, "Big 3 CRM"
- **Revenue claims:** "$60k/month for white-label partners"
- **Partnership references:** "Google-Reddit partnership"
- **Stats:** "97% of buyers", "Reddit now 20% of search"

### 5. Personalization Variables Used
- `{FIRST_NAME}` - Heavy usage throughout
- `{COMPANY}` - Company name
- `{TITLE}` - Job title
- `{CASE_STUDY}` - For agency pitches (SEO firm case studies)
- `{HOOK}` - For gaming (game titles)
- `{SENDER_EMAIL_SIGNATURE}` - Closing signature
- `{SENDER_FIRST_NAME}` - More casual close

### 6. Copy Spin Tactics (Extensive Use)
- **Heavy spintax:** Every campaign uses {option1|option2|option3} extensively
- **Maintains voice:** Variations stay conversational, not robotic
- **Randomization scope:** Verbs, adjectives, phrases (not just synonyms)
- **Natural flow:** Spins read naturally when any combination renders

### 7. Sequence Structure Best Practices
- **Step 1 (Day 0-1):** Short, high-level value prop with curiosity gap
- **Step 2 (Day 3-5):** "Looking back I should've explained better" (self-deprecation + clarity)
- **Step 3 (Day 4-6):** Deep dive on "why now" (partnerships, stats, FOMO) + social proof
- **Step 4 (optional, Day 7-9):** Humor/personality + "who else on team?" (route-around)

### 8. Psychological Triggers
- **Self-deprecation:** "I should've explained better", "Last one was vague"
- **FOMO:** "visibility is easy to capture right now", "your competitors are..."
- **Authority:** Blizzard, Big 3 CRM, "$60k/month"
- **Scarcity (implied):** "10 of these this week" (demand signal)
- **Curiosity:** "weird one", "wild one", incomplete information
- **Social proof clusters:** Multiple types in same email (brand + stat + case study)

### 9. CTA Patterns
- **Low friction:** "let me know either way" (acknowledges no = ok)
- **Super casual:** "lmk", "give me a shout"
- **Binary choice:** "Interested?" vs. long form
- **Route-around:** "or is there someone else on the team?"
- **Call to inaction:** "don't make it weird, lmk either way" (reverse psychology)

### 10. Plain Text Formatting
- **All winning campaigns:** Use plain_text: true
- **No HTML formatting:** Strips away designed emails
- **Conversational:** Feels like real 1:1 email, not marketing blast
- **Open tracking OFF:** All winning campaigns have open_tracking: false
