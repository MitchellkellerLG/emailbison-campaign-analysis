## Executive Summary

New Zealand is in the midst of significant curriculum changes that are creating both urgency and controversy among school principals and teachers. The **updated Years 0-10 curriculum for English, Te Reo Rangatira, Mathematics and Statistics, and PƒÅngarau** was released October 19, 2025, with mandatory implementation starting **Term 1, 2026** (not 2025 as previously required).

**Key finding:** Over 650 principals have signed an open letter urging the Education Minister to **pause the rollout**, citing it as "too much, too fast, without enough support." This creates a critical opportunity for TeachAid to position as the solution to implementation challenges.

## Current State: Principals' Frustrations

### The Open Letter (October 17, 2025)
**650 primary principals signed**, endorsed by:
- **Principals' Federation** (representing 2,000 principals)
- **NZEI union**
- **Te Akatea** (MƒÅori principals' association)

### Key Complaints from Principals

**1. Rushed Implementation Without Tools**
- *"Expectations for rollout of big changes without having the proper tools already ready, like the assessment tools, everything is being built while it's being rolled out"* - Principal Tute Mila, Arakura School
- Principals hear about changes through media first, then scramble to get information
- No adequate time or resourcing

**2. Inadequate Professional Development**
- Only **3 days PLD** for structured literacy
- *"Three days PLD is woefully inadequate to create the kind of shifts in pedagogy"*
- Teachers who accessed PLD report it's useful, but there's not enough of it

**3. One-Size-Fits-All Approach**
- No provision for students with additional learning needs
- Schools developing adaptations themselves
- Uncertainty about adapting for neurodivergent or disabled learners

**4. Changes to Already-Implemented Content**
- Teachers adapted in early 2025 for Years 0-8
- October 2025 release changed that same content again
- *"I feel sorry for the teachers who have already worked on Years 0-8 in maths and 0-6 in English to find their curriculum content has changed"* - Facebook comment

**5. Small/Rural Schools Struggling Most**
- Only **69% of small schools have a plan for implementing English changes**
- Only **78% have someone leading delivery** (vs 90%+ for larger schools)
- Rural schools less likely to access PLD and resources

## What's Actually Happening (ERO Report - Oct 15, 2025)

### Positive Signs
- **98% of schools** have started using refreshed curriculum for English and maths
- **54% of teachers** report improved achievement in English
- **51% of teachers** report improved achievement in maths
- **Phonics checks show significant improvement:**
  - Term 1, 2025: 36% at/above expectation
  - Term 2, 2025: 51% at/above expectation
  - Term 3, 2025: 58% at/above expectation
- **Nearly all teachers (97%+)** are using evidence-backed teaching strategies

### Challenges Teachers Face

**1. Teaching Maths is Harder Than English**
- Teachers focus on **number** across all year levels (even Years 7-8)
- Only 75% teach number "a lot" in Years 7-8, when they should be teaching more algebra/probability/statistics
- Teachers are less confident teaching complex maths content
- Number has been a relative weakness for NZ students

**2. Managing Multi-Level Classrooms**
- Unsure about adapting for multi-year classrooms
- Uncertainty about when/how to escalate support for struggling students
- Want more resources to enable and extend students' learning

**3. Small Schools Still Struggle**
- Only **84% of small schools** have a plan for maths implementation
- Only **69% of small schools** have a plan for English implementation
- Less likely to have someone leading delivery

**4. MƒÅori-Medium Provision Challenges**
- PLD, guidance, and resources don't reflect breadth of settings
- Teachers in rumaki often translate/adapt English-medium resources themselves
- Little change in what/how students are taught in MƒÅori-medium settings

## Key Terminology & Concepts

### Structured Literacy Approaches
**What it is:** Evidence-based approach to teaching reading that emphasizes:
- Phonics (systematic and explicit)
- Phonological awareness
- Vocabulary
- Fluency
- Comprehension
- Writing craft

**Implementation requirement:** Mandatory from Term 1, 2025 for Years 0-6 English

**Teacher training:** 3 days PLD (principals say inadequate)

**Results so far:** Phonics checks show 58% of students at/above expectation (up from 36% in Term 1)

### Explicit Teaching Strategies
**What it is:** Teachers clearly explain concepts, model thinking, and provide structured practice

**Implementation:** Part of maths curriculum changes

**Teacher adoption:** Nearly all teachers (97%+) report using these strategies

### Te MƒÅtaiaho Framework
**What it is:** Underlying curriculum framework that will support all learning areas from 2027

**Purpose:** Nationally consistent framework setting out essential knowledge every student should be taught

### Key MƒÅori Concepts Referenced
- **Mana Whenua:** Connection to land, territorial rights
- **Ako:** Reciprocal teaching and learning
- **MƒÅtauranga MƒÅori:** MƒÅori knowledge and ways of knowing

## Pain Points TeachAid Can Address

### 1. **Unit Planning Overload**
**The Problem:**
- Curriculum changed in early 2025
- Teachers adapted their unit plans
- October 2025 release changed content AGAIN (even for already-implemented years)
- Teachers must rebuild unit plans for Term 1, 2026
- Structured literacy requires different planning approach than before

**TeachAid Solution:**
- Generate curriculum-aligned unit plans in minutes
- Automatically incorporate structured literacy components
- Adapt for multi-year classrooms
- Include MƒÅori-specific teachings (Mana Whenua, Ako)

### 2. **Maths Content Gaps**
**The Problem:**
- Teachers focusing on number (comfort zone) rather than full curriculum
- Less confident teaching algebra, probability, statistics
- Students need foundational support before moving to complex content
- Teachers want more resources to extend learning

**TeachAid Solution:**
- Complete units across ALL maths components (not just number)
- Scaffolded progression (build foundations, then extend)
- Ready-to-use activities for complex topics teachers are less confident with

### 3. **Time Poverty**
**The Problem:**
- 1 hour per day required for reading, writing, maths
- Unit planning takes hours/weekends
- Adapting for diverse learners takes more time
- Small school teachers often teaching multiple year levels

**TeachAid Solution:**
- Generate complete units in minutes (vs hours/days)
- Includes slides, tests, activities ready to deploy
- Customizable for different learner needs

### 4. **Small/Rural School Isolation**
**The Problem:**
- Only 69-84% have implementation plans
- Less access to PLD and peer networks
- Often teaching multiple year levels simultaneously
- Less likely to have curriculum leadership support

**TeachAid Solution:**
- Self-service tool (don't need PLD or peer support)
- Multi-year classroom templates
- Works for schools without dedicated curriculum leaders

### 5. **Assessment Tools Not Ready**
**The Problem:**
- *"Assessment tools, everything is being built while it's being rolled out"*
- Teachers need to assess progress under new curriculum
- Phonics checks required but tools still developing

**TeachAid Solution:**
- Complete units include assessment components
- Tests aligned to curriculum expectations
- Progress tracking built-in

### 6. **MƒÅori-Medium Resource Gap**
**The Problem:**
- PLD/resources don't reflect MƒÅori-medium settings
- Teachers translating/adapting English-medium materials themselves
- Little change happening in rumaki settings

**TeachAid Solution:**
- MƒÅori-specific teachings integrated (Mana Whenua, Ako)
- Can generate content in te reo MƒÅori context
- Designed with MƒÅori education experts
