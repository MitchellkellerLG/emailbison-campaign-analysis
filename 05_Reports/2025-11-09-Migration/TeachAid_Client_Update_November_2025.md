## üéØ New Copy Strategy: Ego-Appeal Positioning

For campaigns targeting **higher-level positions** (principals, administrators, directors), we've implemented a strategic copy shift:

### Old Approach
‚ùå Direct pitch: "Would you like to see TeachAid?"
‚ùå Transactional: Asking for their time/attention
‚ùå One-directional: We want something from them

### New Approach
‚úÖ **Expert feedback request:** "We'd love your expert feedback on..."
‚úÖ **Ego-appeal positioning:** Positioning them as the authority
‚úÖ **Consultative tone:** "We value your insights as an education leader"
‚úÖ **Reason for the call:** Changing from sales to advisory consultation

**Rationale:** Higher-level decision-makers respond better when positioned as experts being consulted rather than prospects being sold to. This approach:
1. Appeals to their professional ego and expertise
2. Lowers resistance by removing sales pressure
3. Positions TeachAid as seeking validation (not selling)
4. Creates reciprocity ("they helped us, now they're invested")

**Implementation:** This strategy is being deployed in principal and administrator campaigns starting this week.
