# üìä KEY INSIGHTS & WINNING ELEMENTS

## What Made Campaign #199 Win (19.5% vs 15.4% vs 6.6% vs 3.7%)

### 1. **Audience Matters**
- **Principals >> Teachers** for reply rates
- Principals (19.5% & 15.4%) vs Teachers (6.6% & 3.7%)
- Principals have budget authority and decision-making power

### 2. **Pain Point Specificity**
- **WINNER:** "new math and English curriculum changes" (specific, timely)
- LOWER: Generic "MƒÅori curricular unit" (less urgent)
- Tying to curriculum rollouts creates immediate need

### 3. **Sequence Length**
- **WINNER:** 4-step sequence
- LOWER: 2-3 step sequences
- More touchpoints = more opportunities to convert

### 4. **Wait Times**
- Campaign #199: Wait 3 days ‚Üí Wait 6 days ‚Üí Wait 1 day
- Longer waits between Steps 2-3 performed better

### 5. **Spintax Density**
- Campaign #199 had the MOST spintax variations
- Helps avoid spam filters over large volumes

### 6. **Workshop Invitation**
- Step 4 "Coffee, food and AI teaching tools" unique to winning campaign
- In-person community building = higher engagement

### 7. **Burnout Angle**
- Teacher burnout variant tested in #199
- Alternative framing for different principal personalities

### 8. **Subject Line Strategy**
- "curriculum rollouts for LATs" >> "MƒÅori curricular unit"
- LAT-specific (decision makers) >> general teacher pitch

### 9. **Volume Advantage**
- Campaign #199: 1,048 leads (larger sample = better optimization)
- Smaller campaigns may not have had time to optimize

## Copy Elements Present in ALL Winners

‚úÖ "weird one" / "odd one" / "crazy question" opener
‚úÖ MƒÅori-specific teachings (Mana Whenua, Ako, Parakau)
‚úÖ Ministry of Education partnership credibility
‚úÖ "in minutes" time savings
‚úÖ 30-day free trial, no strings
‚úÖ "keep everything they create" ownership
‚úÖ Heavy spintax throughout
‚úÖ Plain text, no open tracking

## Why Teachers Campaigns Underperformed

1. **Less decision-making authority** - Can't commit to school-wide tools
2. **Less urgency** - Not responsible for curriculum implementation
3. **Shorter sequences** - Only 2 steps vs 4
4. **Generic positioning** - "MƒÅori unit" less compelling than "curriculum changes"
5. **No workshop invitation** - Less community/social proof angle

# üéØ RECOMMENDATIONS FOR FUTURE CAMPAIGNS

## DO THIS (From Winners):
1. **Target principals/decision makers** not individual teachers
2. **Lead with specific curriculum pain points** not generic benefits
3. **Use 4-step sequences** with strategic wait times (3 days, 6 days, 1 day)
4. **Include workshop/community element** in later steps
5. **Test burnout angle** as variant for different personalities
6. **Heavy spintax** to avoid spam filters at scale
7. **Colleague referral in Step 3** creates viral loop
8. **"weird one" opener** consistently outperforms formal approaches

## AVOID THIS (From Lower Performers):
1. Don't pitch individual teachers (low authority)
2. Don't use generic "would you like X" without specific pain point
3. Don't stop at 2-3 steps (missed conversion opportunities)
4. Don't skip the social proof/workshop invitation
5. Don't forget LAT-specific angle (they're curriculum decision makers)
