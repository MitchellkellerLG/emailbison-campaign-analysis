## Key Success Factors (NZ Principals Campaigns)

### Campaign #199 - Exceptional Performance
- **19.56% reply rate** - Highest performing TeachAid campaign by reply volume
- **3.91% interested rate** - 41 interested principals (decision-makers!)
- **4-step nurture sequence** with workshop invitation closer
- **Multiple A/B test variants** ("weird one" vs "odd one", burnout angle)

### Common Winning Elements

1. **Pattern Interrupt Openings**
   - "weird one" / "odd one" hooks (borrowed from Foundation's winning formula)
   - Immediate value proposition in first sentence

2. **Māori Cultural Integration**
   - Mana Whenua and Ako teachings mentioned prominently
   - Ministry of Education partnership credibility
   - Aotearoa/NZ localization throughout

3. **LAT Focus** (Limited Authority Teachers)
   - Pain point: New curriculum rollout burden on temporary staff
   - Solution: Generate complete units in minutes

4. **Social Proof Layering**
   - "1000s of k-12 educators across Aotearoa already using it"
   - "Hundreds of schools we support"
   - "Principals loving our tool" at workshops

5. **Low-Friction Offers**
   - 30-day free pilot (not trial - implies no commitment)
   - "Keep everything they create" (value retention)
   - Workshop invitations with food/wine (community building)

6. **Referral Mechanisms**
   - {COLLEAGUE_1} variable for horizontal referrals
   - "Someone better on the team" (permission to delegate)
   - Staff-focused offers (taking burden off principal)

7. **Spintax Variations**
   - Extensive variations maintain freshness
   - Multiple subject line tests
   - Conversational tone throughout

### Technical Settings (Both Campaigns)
- **Plain text:** Yes
- **Open tracking:** No
- **Thread replies:** Yes (maintains conversation flow)
- **Wait times:** 1-6 days between touches
- **Throttle:** Conservative (500/day max)
