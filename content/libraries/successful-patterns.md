## Opening Hook Patterns (From Real Tweets)

### Pattern 0: Personal Decision Hook (Mitchell's Style)
**Format:** "[Blunt personal action/decision]."

**Real Example:**
"Just said no to a deal cause I was getting bad vibes."

**Why it works:**
- Ultra-direct, no fluff
- Shows positioning/selectivity
- Relatable decision-making
- Invites reader into mindset

**When to use:** Personal Updates, Authority Building

**Variations:**
- "Fired a client this morning."
- "Turned down $X offer because [reason]."
- "Said yes to [unconventional thing] and here's why."

### Pattern 1: Common Mistake Hook
**Format:** "Most [people/teams/agencies] do [X]. Here's the problem: [consequence]."

**Why it works:** Immediately identifies with reader's likely mistake, creates curiosity about the better way.

**Examples:**
- "Most B2B teams target championsâ€”mid-level folks who love the product. Here's the problem: champions don't have budget authority."
- "Most agencies optimize copy but never fix the targeting. They're running A/B tests on the wrong audience."
- "Most teams pick one subject line and blast it. None of that matters if you're targeting the wrong people."

**When to use:** Tactical Guides, Strategy Guides, Objection Handling

**Voice note:** Follow with specific alternative + proof. Don't just criticize without teaching.

### Pattern 2: Result-First Hook
**Format:** "We just [achieved result]. Here's what made the difference: [insight]."

**Why it works:** Leads with credibility-building proof, then provides the lesson.

**Examples:**

**When to use:** Client Success Stories, Case Studies

**Voice note:** Always include context for the result (industry average, previous performance, or "vs typical X%").

### Pattern 3: Objection Statement Hook
**Format:** "[Direct quote of objection]."

**Why it works:** Immediately resonates with readers who've heard or said this exact thing.

**Voice note:** State objection verbatim (in quotes), then address it systematically.

## Call-to-Action Patterns

### Pattern 1: Soft Positioning (80/20 Rule)
**Format:** End with takeaway, not hard CTA

**Examples:**
- "Fix the targeting first. Everything else is easier after that."
- "Test positioning, not just phrasing."
- "Most teams never figure this out. They optimize copy on the wrong audience."

**Why it works:** Leaves reader with value, positions authority without selling

**When to use:** Top/Middle of Funnel (Tactical Guides, Success Stories, Objection Handling)

---

### Pattern 2: Implied Next Step
**Format:** "If [situation], [implied action]."

**Examples:**
- "If you're targeting a market below 20K companies, you're probably wasting time."
- "If you're stuck at <5% reply rates, you're likely targeting the wrong level."
- "This works for B2B companies with LTV $5K+ and clear ICP."

**Why it works:** Reader self-qualifies, can reach out if it fits

**When to use:** Middle/Bottom of Funnel (Strategy Guides, Case Studies)

---

### Pattern 3: Direct CTA (Product Launch Only)
**Format:** "DM me [keyword] if [qualifier]."

**Examples:**
- "DM me 'AUDIT' if interestedâ€”first 3 committed get priority."
- "Reach out if you're stuck at <5% reply rates with LTV $10K+ product."

**Why it works:** Clear action, qualified audience, scarcity if appropriate

**When to use:** Product Launch / Giveaway posts ONLY (with Mitchell approval)
