# Alberta K-12 Education Landscape Research Report
## TeachAid Market Intelligence - October 2025

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Alberta presents a **HIGH OPPORTUNITY** market for TeachAid with significant curriculum transformation underway, strong Indigenous education mandates, and documented teacher capacity challenges. The province is in the midst of a comprehensive K-6 curriculum renewal that started in 2019, with mandatory implementation rolling out through 2026.

**Overall Market Opportunity Score: 8.5/10**

---

## 1. CURRICULUM OVERHAULS
**Impact Score: 9/10**

### Current State
Alberta is implementing one of the most comprehensive curriculum overhauls in Canadian K-12 education:

- **K-6 Curriculum Renewal** launched in 2019
- **Completed subjects** (ready for implementation):
  - English language arts and literature
  - French first language and literature
  - French immersion language arts
  - Mathematics
  - Physical education and wellness
  - Science

- **Social Studies** implementation timeline:
  - September 2025: Mandatory for K-3 classrooms
  - September 2026: Mandatory for grades 4-6 classrooms

### Key Changes
- **"Refocusing on essential knowledge"** - back-to-basics approach
- Emphasis on **specific knowledge and foundational skills**
- Updated to reflect modern pedagogy standards
- Complete restructuring of learning outcomes across all subjects

### Teacher Impact
- Teachers facing **multiple simultaneous curriculum implementations**
- Need to develop entirely new lesson plans, activities, and assessments
- Limited prep time between announcement and mandatory implementation
- **High stress** around ensuring alignment with new standards

**Marketing Hook:** Teachers are overwhelmed with creating curriculum-aligned materials for subjects they're implementing for the first time in September 2025/2026.

## 2. INDIGENOUS/CULTURAL REQUIREMENTS
**Impact Score: 9/10**

### Truth and Reconciliation Commission Mandates
Alberta has committed to implementing TRC Calls to Action in education:

- **Mandatory Indigenous content integration** across all subjects
- First Nations, Métis, and Inuit perspectives required in curriculum
- Focus on eliminating funding discrepancies in Indigenous education
- Addressing historical context of residential schools

### Specific Requirements
- Indigenous knowledge systems must be woven throughout curriculum
- Treaty education requirements
- Land acknowledgment and territorial awareness
- Indigenous language preservation efforts

### Implementation Challenges
- Many teachers lack training in Indigenous pedagogy
- Uncertainty about how to **authentically integrate** Indigenous content
- Fear of cultural appropriation or getting it wrong
- Need for resources that properly represent Indigenous perspectives

### Cultural Considerations
- Alberta has **8 First Nations language groups** and significant Métis populations
- Growing emphasis on **reconciliation education**
- Schools expected to incorporate local Indigenous protocols

**Marketing Hook:** Alberta teachers need curriculum materials that authentically integrate First Nations, Métis, and Inuit perspectives to meet TRC mandates - especially with the new social studies curriculum launching in 2025.

---

## 3. TEACHER SHORTAGE/CRISIS
**Impact Score: 7/10**

### Current Situation
Alberta faces moderate-to-significant teacher workforce challenges:

- **Teacher retention crisis**: 50% of teachers leave within first 2 years
- Early career attrition driven by:
  - Difficulty balancing work and home life
  - Time constraints (especially for lesson planning)
  - Classroom management challenges
  - Feeling incompetent or under-prepared

### Strike Action & Labor Issues
- **Alberta Teachers' Association on strike** (effective October 2024)
- Rejected government's "biased mediation proposal"
- Teachers advocating for better working conditions
- **Alberta spends the least on public education per-student in Canada**

### Capacity Challenges
- Schools struggling to fill specialized positions
- Increased reliance on substitute teachers
- Teacher burnout due to curriculum changes + workload
- **"Stop the Excuses"** campaign highlighting funding gaps

### Impact on Curriculum Implementation
- Overwhelmed teachers have **less time** to develop quality curriculum materials
- New teachers entering during massive curriculum transition
- Limited professional development time
- Need for **time-saving tools** is critical

**Marketing Hook:** With Alberta investing the least per student in Canada and teachers on strike over workload, time-saving curriculum tools that help them implement new standards in minutes (not days) are essential.

## 4. MULTILINGUAL CONSIDERATIONS

### French Language Education

- New French immersion language arts curriculum completed
- French first language programs for Francophone communities
- Need for bilingual curriculum resources

### English as Additional Language (EAL/ESL)
- Growing immigrant population in urban centers (Calgary, Edmonton)
- Diverse language backgrounds requiring ESL support
- Teachers need **adaptable materials** for different language levels
- Newcomer integration challenges

### Language Diversity
- 200+ languages spoken in Alberta schools
- High concentration in Calgary and Edmonton school divisions
- Teachers managing classrooms with 10+ first languages represented

### Indigenous Languages
- 8 First Nations language groups
- Cree, Blackfoot, Dene, and others
- Growing movement for Indigenous language revitalization in schools

**Marketing Hook:** Alberta teachers need curriculum materials that can be adapted for French immersion classrooms, EAL learners, and Indigenous language programs - especially with the multicultural makeup of Calgary and Edmonton schools.

## 5. SCHOOL PERFORMANCE ISSUES
**Impact Score: 6/10**

### Standardized Test Concerns
- **PISA scores declining** - causing provincial concern
- Provincial Achievement Tests in grades 6 and 9
- Grade 12 diploma examinations
- Pressure to improve performance while implementing new curriculum

### Current Challenges
- **Grade inflation** concerns
- Debates over curriculum teaching methods
- Class size issues
- Public vs. private school funding controversies

### Implementation Stress
- Schools judged on test scores **during curriculum transition**
- Teachers worried about student performance with new materials
- Need to maintain outcomes while learning new curriculum
- Accountability pressure during major change period

### Intervention Landscape
- While not state takeovers like in Ohio, schools face:
  - School improvement plans
  - District oversight for underperformance
  - Public reporting of achievement data

**Marketing Hook:** Schools need to maintain or improve test scores while implementing entirely new curricula - TeachAid can help teachers create standards-aligned materials that support student achievement during this transition.
