# Ohio K-12 Education Landscape Research Report
## TeachAid Market Intelligence - October 2025

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Ohio presents a **STRONG OPPORTUNITY** market for TeachAid with documented teacher shortages, history of state intervention in struggling districts, curriculum standards updates, and diverse urban/rural education challenges.

**Overall Market Opportunity Score: 8/10**

---

## 1. CURRICULUM OVERHAULS
**Impact Score: 7/10**

### Ohio's Learning Standards
Ohio has updated curriculum standards across subjects:

- **Ohio's Learning Standards** replace older standards
- Recent revisions in:
  - English Language Arts
  - Mathematics
  - Science
  - Social Studies
- **Common Core** transition and adaptation
- Career-technical education integration

### Standards Implementation
- Districts must align curriculum to state standards
- **State testing** (Ohio State Tests) aligned to standards
- Pressure for curriculum materials that match standards
- Third Grade Reading Guarantee - high-stakes literacy

### STEM Emphasis
- Focus on **STEM education**
- Computer science standards
- Technology integration requirements
- Career readiness pathways

### Local Control vs. State Standards
- Ohio has **strong local control tradition**
- Districts can choose curriculum materials
- Must meet state standards but implementation varies
- Creates market for vetted, standards-aligned resources

**Marketing Hook:** Ohio teachers need curriculum materials aligned with Ohio's Learning Standards that prepare students for state testing while maintaining local flexibility in implementation.

## 2. INDIGENOUS/CULTURAL REQUIREMENTS
**Impact Score: 4/10**

### Native American Education
Limited but growing requirements:

- **Native American Heritage Month** recognition (November)
- Some districts have Native American education requirements
- Growing awareness of:
  - Historical treatment of Indigenous peoples
  - Contemporary Native American issues
  - Local tribal histories

### Ohio's Indigenous Context
- Historical home to **multiple Native nations** (Shawnee, Miami, Delaware, Wyandot, others)
- Modern tribal presence limited (no federally recognized tribes in Ohio)
- **American Indian Education Program** exists in some urban districts

### Multicultural Education
- **Diversity, equity, inclusion** requirements vary by district
- Urban districts (Cleveland, Columbus, Cincinnati) emphasize multicultural ed
- Rural areas less focus
- Growing awareness post-2020

### Cultural Considerations
- **Appalachian culture** in southeastern Ohio
- Rust Belt industrial heritage
- Agricultural/rural cultural identity
- Urban diversity in major cities

**Marketing Hook:** While not as intensive as Canadian Indigenous requirements, Ohio teachers (especially in urban districts) need resources that address diverse cultural perspectives and historically marginalized communities.

---

## 3. TEACHER SHORTAGE/CRISIS
**Impact Score: 9/10** ⭐ **CRITICAL ISSUE**

### Severe Teacher Shortage
Ohio faces one of the **worst teacher shortages in the United States**:

- **Thousands of unfilled teaching positions** annually
- Heavy use of **substitute teachers** in long-term roles
- Emergency teaching licenses/permits
- **Declining enrollment** in teacher preparation programs
- Exodus from profession (especially post-COVID)

### High-Need Areas
Critical shortages in:
- **Mathematics and Science** - severe, ongoing
- **Special Education** - desperate need
- **English Language Learner (ELL)** teachers
- **Career-Technical Education**
- Rural districts - all subjects
- Urban districts - high turnover

### Teacher Retention Crisis
- **High turnover** especially in urban districts
- Cleveland, Columbus, Cincinnati lose teachers rapidly
- Low salaries compared to neighboring states
- **Difficult working conditions** in struggling districts
- Limited support for new teachers
- 50%+ leave within 5 years in some districts

### Salary and Compensation
- **Low teacher pay** compared to national average
- Salary compression - little growth for experienced teachers
- Benefits cuts in many districts
- Need second jobs to survive

### Uncertified Teachers
- **Emergency permits** widely used
- Subject-area permits for non-certified teachers
- Long-term substitutes teaching core subjects
- Quality concerns

**Marketing Hook:** With thousands of unfilled positions and emergency-certified teachers filling Ohio classrooms, tools that help ANY teacher (certified or not) deliver quality, standards-aligned instruction are desperately needed.

## 4. MULTILINGUAL CONSIDERATIONS
**Impact Score: 7/10**

### English Language Learners (ELL)
Growing ELL population creates challenges:

- **Increasing immigrant and refugee** populations
- Major cities (Columbus, Cleveland, Cincinnati, Akron) have significant ELL students
- **Over 200 languages** spoken across Ohio schools
- Limited ELL-certified teachers

### Language Communities
Major language groups:
- **Spanish** - largest ELL population
- **Somali** - significant refugee community (Columbus)
- **Arabic** - refugee and immigrant populations
- **Nepali** - growing community
- **Burmese** - refugee population
- **Chinese, Vietnamese** - established communities

### ELL Support Challenges
- Insufficient ELL specialists
- Mainstream teachers **lack ELL training**
- Need for differentiated instruction
- Limited bilingual resources
- Assessment challenges for ELL students

### Bilingual Education
- Limited **true bilingual programs**
- Mostly English immersion with ELL support
- Spanish-English programs in some urban districts
- Heritage language programs rare

**Marketing Hook:** Ohio teachers (especially in Columbus, Cleveland, Akron) are teaching students from 200+ language backgrounds with minimal ELL training - they need curriculum materials adaptable for different English proficiency levels.

## 5. SCHOOL PERFORMANCE ISSUES
**Impact Score: 10/10** ⭐ **DEFINING CHARACTERISTIC**

### Academic Distress Commissions (Historical)
Ohio had **notorious state takeover system**:

- **Academic Distress Commissions** for lowest-performing districts
- State takeovers of:
  - Youngstown City Schools
  - Lorain City Schools
  - East Cleveland Schools
- Highly controversial, largely ended
- Created culture of **fear and accountability**

### Current Accountability System
- **Ohio School Report Cards** - public A-F ratings
- Schools rated on:
  - Achievement
  - Progress
  - Gap Closing
  - Graduation Rate
  - Early Literacy
- **High-stakes accountability**

### Struggling Districts
Major challenges in:
- **Urban districts** - Cleveland, Columbus, Cincinnati, Dayton, Toledo, Youngstown
- Post-industrial cities - economic decline affecting schools
- **Rural Appalachian** districts - poverty, limited resources
- High poverty correlates with low performance

### Third Grade Reading Guarantee
- **Students must pass reading test** to advance to 4th grade
- High pressure on K-3 teachers
- Retention threatens students
- Intensive intervention requirements

### State Testing Pressure
- **Ohio State Tests** (OST) - grades 3-8
- End-of-Course exams for high school
- Results determine school ratings
- Teacher evaluations tied to test scores (varies by district)

### Intervention and Support
- Schools with low ratings require improvement plans
- State support teams
- **Threat of takeover** (though policy changed)
- Pressure to show rapid improvement

**Marketing Hook:** Ohio schools face extreme accountability pressure with A-F report cards and high-stakes testing - teachers need proven, effective curriculum materials that drive student achievement and protect their schools from intervention.
