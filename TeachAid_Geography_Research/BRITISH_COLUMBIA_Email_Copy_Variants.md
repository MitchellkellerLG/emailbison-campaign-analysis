## CAMPAIGN NOTES

### Key Differences Between Variants (3 Total - Matching Ontario Structure)
- **Variant A**: FPPL-first approach - Indigenous pedagogy as foundation, not add-on
- **Variant B**: Multilingual diversity focus - 250+ languages in Metro Vancouver, ELL support
- **Variant C**: Big Ideas & Core Competencies alignment - Competency-based curriculum framework

### BC-Specific Elements Used
- **FPPL** (First Peoples Principles of Learning) - BC curriculum foundation, mentioned explicitly
- **FNESC** (First Nations Education Steering Committee) - official partnership for credibility
- **Big Ideas and Core Competencies** - BC curriculum framework (exact terminology)
- **ELL** (English Language Learning) - BC's official term for language learners
- **School districts** (not boards) - correct BC administrative structure
- **Specific BC districts for social proof**: Surrey Schools, Richmond School District
- **Metro Vancouver, Lower Mainland** - geographic specificity
- **250+ languages statistic** - Metro Vancouver concrete data
- **Specific languages**: Mandarin, Punjabi, Tagalog, Farsi (major BC language groups)

### Value Density Elements (Matching Ontario)
- **"for every teacher at {SCHOOL_NAME} for free"** - concrete, specific offer
- **"Armed with..."** - tangible deliverables listed upfront
- **"Either way, let me know"** - low-pressure close
- **"Not your thing? reply 'recess'"** - easy opt-out every email
- **Social proof with district names** - "Surrey Schools," "Richmond School District"
- **Time comparison** - "coffee break instead of an entire weekend"
- **"They can keep everything they create"** - ownership emphasis
- **Multiple integration points** - "Google Classroom and other systems"
- **Shorter paragraphs** - easier to scan
- **Concrete examples** - specific languages, specific tools

### Subject Line Spintax (2-4 words, chill)
- **Variant A**: FPPL curriculum units | curriculum with FPPL | FPPL-based units
- **Variant B**: ELL curriculum units | multilingual classroom support | diverse classroom units

### Format Changes from Previous Version
- Removed long-winded introductions
- Added specific school/district merge fields {SCHOOL_NAME}
- Added concrete deliverable lists ("Armed with...")
- Added social proof with real district names
- Added "Not your thing? reply 'recess'" to every email
- Simplified P.S. structure
- Added time comparison in Step 2
- Shortened sentences throughout

### Principal's Perspective - Value Propositions
Both variants now emphasize:
1. **Free, specific offer** - Unit for every teacher, by name
2. **Concrete deliverables** - Not vague promises
3. **Social proof** - Real BC districts using it
4. **Time savings** - Coffee break vs. weekend
5. **Ownership** - Keep everything created
6. **BC authenticity** - FPPL, ELL, Big Ideas terminology
7. **Low pressure** - Easy opt-out option
